What's New in Vektor: March Update

🧾 1099 Report

Preparing 1099s for owner-operators used to mean reconciling settlements by hand. Not anymore.

Assign a tax classification to each settlement category — Taxable Income (1099-NEC), Deduction, Advance Recovery, and more. Vektor then generates a complete report: every driver, every pay period, every settlement — aggregated and ready to export.

How to use

Settlements → 1099 Report. Start with Configuration to classify your categories, then Preview to review and download.

✉️ Mail Templates for Dispatch Sheets & Rate Confirmations

You can now create fully customizable email templates for Rate Confirmations and Dispatch Sheets — with a subject line, body, and dynamic variables that auto-fill with real load data: carrier name, lane, origin, destination, rate total, dispatcher info, and more.

Assign a template to a specific carrier — and when you hit Send on any load, Vektor automatically preselects the right one based on who's assigned. No switching, no copy-paste.

A live preview shows exactly how the email will render with real data before anything goes out.

How to use

Go to Settings → Mailing → Mail Templates, create a template, choose Rate Confirmation or Dispatch Sheet as the category, and assign it to the relevant carriers.

📋 Manifest Expense Items

Not every cost on a load shows up on the customer invoice — but it still hits your margin.

Toll reimbursements, lumper fees, fuel advances, and other operational expenses can now be tracked directly on the manifest as Expense Items. They don't appear on rate confirmations or customer invoices — that's intentional. They live at the manifest level, reflected in manifest reports and your internal cost picture.

The result: dispatchers and ops managers finally see what a load actually costs to run — not just what it billed.

📅 Driver YTD on Settlement Statements

Come tax season, drivers ask one question more than any other: what did I make this year?

Settlement statements now include each driver's Year-to-Date gross earnings, calculated from the start of the calendar year and updated automatically with every closed period. The number is right on the statement — no calls to accounting, no manual exports, no back-and-forth.

For owner-operators especially, this is the number that goes straight into their tax prep.

🚫 Document Compliance Check on Truck Assignment

A truck without valid documents isn't just a paperwork problem — it's a liability on wheels.

When assigning a truck to a manifest, dispatchers now see an immediate alert if required documents are missing, expired, or expiring soon. Whether it's registration, insurance, or a driver license — Vektor flags it before the assignment goes through. A quick "I acknowledge" confirmation lets the dispatcher proceed when they choose to, with full awareness of the gap.

Every truck assignment is now a built-in compliance checkpoint — not something you discover at a weigh station.

How to use

Configure which document types are required in Settings → Document Types. Mark any type as Required — Vektor enforces it at every truck assignment.

📝 Revenue Type Notes

Revenue Types power your pay logic — but when someone changes one, it's rarely obvious why or when it happened.

Every change to a Revenue Type is now logged as a Note automatically — who made it and when. You can also add a description to any Revenue Type explaining the intent behind it, so new dispatchers and accountants don't have to guess. Less tribal knowledge, more shared context.

How to use

Go to Settings → Revenue Types to add notes and descriptions.
